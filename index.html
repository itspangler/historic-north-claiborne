The Story of North Claiborne Avenue, New Orleans, LA

If you were standing at the intersection of Ursulines and North Claiborne in 1947, you'd be struck by a few things. Clocking in at 191-feet across, the sheer width of the street would be breathtaking. Its massive neutral ground – a local term for what folks in the US generally call a "median" – would be lined by a row of live oak trees, verdant and generously shaded. In New Orleans, North Claiborne Avenue has long been a bustling hub of Black business and local African American culture. This remains true today, as Claiborne is a popular gathering spot during second line parades. However, the space itself is significantly different.
